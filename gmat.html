GMAT

The Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT) is a standardized exam used globally by business schools to assess the qualifications of applicants for advanced study in business and management. The GMAT measures analytical writing, quantitative reasoning, verbal reasoning, and integrated reasoning skills, providing a reliable predictor of academic performance in MBA and other graduate business programs. Administered by the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC), the test is widely accepted by over 7,000 programs at approximately 2,300 graduate business schools worldwide.

GMAT Sections

Analytical Writing Assessment (AWA):

  • Content: One essay prompt

  • Duration: 30 minutes

  • Focus: Tests your ability to analyze an argument and communicate your ideas effectively in writing

Integrated Reasoning (IR):

  • Content: 12 questions

  • Duration: 30 minutes

  • Focus: Assesses your ability to evaluate information from multiple formats and sources, including graphics interpretation, multi-source reasoning, table analysis, and two-part analysis

Quantitative Reasoning:

  • Content: 31 multiple-choice questions

  • Duration: 62 minutes

  • Focus: Measures your quantitative reasoning, problem-solving skills, and ability to interpret graphic data, including problem-solving and data sufficiency questions

Verbal Reasoning:

  • Content: 36 multiple-choice questions

  • Duration: 65 minutes

  • Focus: Tests your ability to read and comprehend material, evaluate arguments, and correct written English. This section includes reading comprehension, critical reasoning, and sentence correction questions
